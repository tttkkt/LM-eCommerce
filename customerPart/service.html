Full Coverage

This typically refers to a comprehensive insurance plan that includes third-party liability, collision, and comprehensive coverages.
Third-Party Liability Insurance

This is the most basic type of insurance, typically legally required as the minimum coverage. It covers damages caused to third parties, including bodily injury and property damage.
Collision Coverage

This insurance covers damages to your own vehicle in the event of a traffic accident, whether it's a collision with another vehicle or a fixed object.
Comprehensive Coverage

This insurance covers losses caused by non-collision events, such as theft, fire, natural disasters, and vandalism.
Personal Injury Protection (PIP)

This insurance covers medical expenses, income loss, and other related costs for the driver and passengers injured in an accident, regardless of fault.
Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage

This insurance provides compensation for you and your passengers if the other driver is uninsured or underinsured and cannot cover your losses.
Medical Payments Coverage (MedPay)

This insurance covers medical expenses for the driver and passengers injured in an accident, regardless of fault.
Roadside Assistance Coverage

This insurance provides emergency services such as towing, battery jump-starts, tire changes, and lockout services.
Loss of Use Coverage

This insurance pays for alternative transportation costs, such as rental cars or public transport, when your vehicle is being repaired.
